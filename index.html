My name is Sasha Bates. I am a current sophomore at the Commonwealth School. I am 15 years old, and attended The Sage School in Foxboro, MA before attending Commonwealth. I am taking seven courses this year: chemistry 2, multivariable calculus, english, medieval world history, bible as history, ap spanish 3, and, of course, computer science. Outside of school, I participate in Model United Nations, Model Congress, debate, math team, and the school theater program.

Commonwealth

Commonwealth is a small, independent coed high school in Boston's Back Bay. It is a school for curious, motivated students looking for challenging academics. The main feature of Commonwealth is its academic rigour. Its students have genuine curiosity, and want to excel not just for grades, but because they want to learn. The school is also situated in two former private homes on Comm Ave, and both the small physical size of the school and the small number of students enrolled mean that teachers personally know every student, and all students knows each other. Teachers are available for help and feedback, and have few enough students that they are very motivated to make sure every student succeeds.

Why I Chose Commonwealth

I didn't originally want to apply to Commonwealth. I thought it would be nerdy and weird, and the academics couldn't be good enough to make up for that. But I was wrong. From the first time I walked into the school, I was blown away by the teachers' knowledge of and passion for their subjects. Our teachers want to be teaching, which makes classes interesting and inspires students to learn. At the first open house I attended, I visited Alan Letarte's geometry mini-class and listened to him explain the Three Great Problems of Antiquity (trisecting an angle, squaring the circle, and duplicating the cube with only a compass and straightedge) and I knew this was the high school I wanted to attend.
